The Displacement Has Already Begun

We are witnessing the onset of the largest workforce displacement in human history. For the first time, our species shares the planet with something capable of outperforming us at cognitive tasks—and it's improving exponentially.

The companies racing to build more capable AI infrastructure are moving faster than society can adapt. With current technology alone—not future breakthroughs—the majority of office work could be automated within a decade. That's over 60 million American jobs at stake.

This isn't fear-mongering. It's arithmetic. The numbers on this page aren't projections of some distant future—they're estimates of displacement already happening, derived from real labor statistics.

We must be clear-eyed about this reality. Society needs to come together—workers, policymakers, and yes, technologists—to defend livelihoods, reimagine education, and build systems that allow humans to adapt and thrive alongside AI, not be discarded by it.
